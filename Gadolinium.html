Discovered by : J.C. Galissard de Marignac

Year Discovered : 1880

Isolated in Geneva, Switzerland

Origin : The element is named after J. Gadolin, a Finnish chemist.

Description :
A silvery metal which reacts with oxygen and water. It is employed as an alloy for making magnets and electronic components. It is also used in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI).
