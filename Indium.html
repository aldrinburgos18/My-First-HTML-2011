Discovered by : F. Reich and H. Richter

Year Discovered : 1863

Isolated in Göttingen, Germany

Origin : The element is named after indigo, which is the colour of the brightest line in its spectrum.

Description :
A soft, silvery metal that is stable and used in low-melting alloys for fire-sprinkler systems in shops and warehouses. Some semi-conductors and transistors are made of indium and arsenic or indium and antimony.
